About me I am a data scientist and HCI researcher who spent four years studying how people learn, adapt, and make decisions when working with AI systems. My PhD work ranged from designing adaptive ML systems for motor learning to investigating how domain experts perceive and challenge generative AI — two sides of the same question: not just whether an AI system performs, but whether it performs fairly, and whether the people it affects can make sense of it. That question shapes how I approach data work. I am drawn to problems where the measurement is hard, the stakes are human, and getting it right requires both statistical rigor and an understanding of behavior. I am currently looking for roles at the intersection of data science, AI evaluation, and behavioral research — in teams that care about building systems that are not just accurate, but meaningful.

Education & previous experience

I hold a PhD in Human-Computer Interaction from Sorbonne University and a Master's degree in Computer Science from Université Grenoble Alpes. My earlier projects ranged from optimizing virtual camera trajectories for electric air taxi (also known as VTOLs) flight videos to building eye-tracking prototypes that improved search relevance. Fun fact: I got into Computer Science because I wanted to create Pixar-style animations.
Prior to my master's, I worked as a Salesforce consultant at Spoon Consulting in Mauritius, supporting digital transformation for clients like AXA and L'Oréal.
